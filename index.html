My First Car - Orange 1972 VW Beetle

Great car. Reliable - so few goodies that there was very little that could go wrong. No AC; manual transmission; manual widows, locks,etc. Great fun to drive.

VW Rabbit*

Just as bare-bones as the Beetle, but far from reliable. This car was noisy and uncomfortable and in constant need of expensive repairs.

Datson/Nissan Maxima Wagon

My boss didn't think much of my VW Rabbit so he offered me a $200 per month car allowance if I would buy something else. I bought a used Maxima Wagon that was a HUGE step up for me. It was super luxurious compared to what I had driven. Cushy fabric seats; power windows and doors; nice stereo; great ride; and very reliable. I don't remember ever taking it to a mechanic.

The First of Three Chrysler Minivans

My husband talked me into buying this first minivan. Shortly fterward, I regretted allowing him to influence me, but it wasn't long before I was appreciating all it had to offer. Although I've owned other vehicles over the last 25 years, I have never been without a minivan. They've been great for chauffering kids and teenagers and their friends; hauling building materials for projects; picking up large purchases; vacations; and hauling merchandise from my various businesses to selling venues. I can't imagine the level of inconvenience of not owning a minivan.

VW New Beetle TDI

An acquaintance suggested that this was my mid-life crisis vehicle. Whatever. It was fun, cute, and had the little luxuries that the original beatles lacked. And the diesel engine meant that I got 40+ mpg...plus diesel was cheaper than gas at that time. Unfortunately, it didn't have the reliability of the original. When my daughter got her driver's license, this became the car she drove.

Four Wheel Dive Ford Ranger

This vehicle was purchased for my daughter's use but I drove it for a few months first. It had massive tires and I had to climb to get in it. We had some fun with this vehicle, including one of my childrens' and my favorite memories; I woke them at 3am on the night of the big ice storm and we drove all over town looking at the storm damage.

Brand-New 2008 Hyundai Elantra Limited with Leather Seats

This was my first-ever brand-new car. Although it was small and fuel-efficient, it had nearly all the bells and whistles at a great price. My daughter still drives this car and loves it. She has never had to have it serviced other than maintenance.

White Hail-Damaged Acura TL

This car was purchased as a vehicle for my daughter to use but I drove it for awhile first and fell in love with it. Instead, I let her have my much newer Elantra. Even though the Acurs was ten years old, I drove it for four years without having to do anything other than change the oil. Plus, after four years, I sold it for more than I paid for it. It was a wonderful car to drive and I really didn't care about the extensive hail damage. In fact, we had two more bad hail storms after I bought it. No worries,

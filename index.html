A Biographical History of Vehicle Ownership

by Betsy Twombly


My First Car - 1974 Orange VW Bug

Great car and very reliable. As my father used to say, "the less it has on it, the less there is to break." So, not much here to break. No AC; no real heater; manual transmission; manual widows, locks, etc. But such great fun to drive. I should also mention one of my favorite things about the old bugs...the front window vents. I still miss those vents.






Volkswagen Rabbit*

Just as bare-bones as the Beetle, but far from reliable. And so ugly! This car was noisy and uncomfortable and in constant need of expensive repairs. It did still have the window vents, which is helpful when you have no air conditioning, but they didn't function quite as nicely as the ones on the bug.






1984 Datsun/Nissan Maxima Wagon

My boss at the time didn't think much of my VW Rabbit, so he offered me a $200 per month car allowance if I would buy something else. I bought a used Maxima Wagon which was a HUGE step up for me. It was super luxurious compared to what I had driven in the past. Cushy fabric seats; power windows and doors; nice stereo; great ride; and very reliable. The boss didn't like it. Oh well. 1984 was the transition year for the Datsun/Nissan name change. This car, like all cars manufactured in 1984, carried both the Datsun and Nissan labels.




Chrysler Grand Caravan Woody

The first of three Chrysler minivans I've owned, my husband talked me into buying this one. Immediately after I bought this I came down with a bad case of buyer's remorse, but it wasn't long before I was appreciating all that a minivan had to offer. Although I've owned and driven other vehicles over the last 25 years, I have never been without a minivan. They've been great for vacations; chauffering kids and teenagers and their friends; hauling equipment and building materials for projects; picking up large purchases; and hauling merchandise from my various businesses to various selling venues. I can't imagine the inconvenience of not owning a minivan.






1997 Teal Chrysler Grand Caravan

My second minivan was only one year old when I purchased it, so it was as close to a new car as I had ever had. I owned this van for about 16 years and put about 130,000 miles on it. It served my family and me well, and I have fond memories of my children associated with it. In it's later years it was an extra vehicle, coming in handy when needed.






2001 VW New Beetle TDI

An acquaintance suggested that this was my mid-life crisis vehicle. Whatever. It was fun, cute, and had the little luxuries that the original beatles lacked. And the diesel engine meant that I got 40+ mpg...plus diesel was cheaper than gas at that time. Unfortunately, it didn't have the reliability of the original. When my daughter got her driver's license, this became the car she drove.






Four Wheel Dive Ford Ranger

This vehicle was purchased for my daughter's use but I drove it for a few months first. It had massive tires and I had to climb to get in it. We had some fun with this vehicle, including one of my childrens' and my favorite memories; I woke them at 3:00 AM on the night of the big ice storm and we drove all over town looking at the storm damage. Other than a few emergency vehicles, we were the only people on the road. With the kids bundled up on top of their pajamas, the eery quiet everywhere we went, and the lights glowing through the coat of ice covering everything, it was absolutely magical.




Brand-New 2008 Hyundai Elantra Limited

This was my first-ever brand-new car. Although it was small and fuel-efficient (the price of gas at that time was nearly $4.00 per gallon), it had nearly all the bells and whistles at a great price. My daughter still drives this car and has been very happy with it. She has never had to have it serviced other than scheduled maintenance.










2002 White Hail-Damaged Acura TL

This is another car that was purchased as a vehicle for my daughter to use, but of course I had to drive it for awhile first. I liked it so much I just couldn't give it up. Instead, I let her have my much newer Elantra. Even though the Acura was 9 years old when I got it, I drove it for four more years without having to do anything other than change the oil. Plus, after four years, my son sold it for me for more than I paid for it. It was a wonderful car to drive and I really didn't care about the extensive hail damage. In fact, we had two more bad hail storms after I bought it. No worries.




2005 Silver Chrysler Town and Country

My third minivan, and nicest, and my current vehicle. Heated leather seats are an option that has become important to me in a car. In order to get the leather seats you generally have to get a top-of-the-line model, which is okay with me. But some of the extras are wasted on me. I nearly lost this vehicle very recently. Someone broke in and attempted to steal it while it was parked on Bayard next to Launchcode.

* My memory is not clear on a few model years. On those vehicles I chose to leave the year off rather than guess incorrectly.
